Quality Check

Material

The material used for the tiles can affect its durability, resistance to wear and tear, and overall appearance. Common materials for tiles include ceramic, porcelain, natural stone, and glass.

Size and Shape

The size and shape of tiles can have an impact on the overall look and feel of a space.

Color and Design

The color and design of the tiles are essential for determining how well they match the style of the space. The pattern of the tile can also affect the visual appeal of the room.

Finish

The finish of the tile can impact its slip resistance and overall durability. Common finishes include matte, glossy, and textured.

Thickness

The thickness of the tile can affect its strength and durability. Thicker tiles are usually more durable and less likely to break or crack.

Water Absorption

The water absorption rate of the tile is essential to consider when choosing tiles for areas that are frequently exposed to moisture, such as bathrooms or kitchens.

Resistance to Stains

The tiles' ability to resist stains is important, especially in high traffic areas where spills are likely to occur.
